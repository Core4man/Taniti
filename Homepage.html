About Taniti

Taniti is a small island in the Pacific with an area of less than 500 square miles. Despite this, it has many sandy and rocky beaches, a safe harbor, lush tropical rainforest, and an active volcano with many mountains surrounding it. It boasts a population of 20,000 people and has become a new place for tourists to come and have vacations. Along with tourism, Taniti's economy is made up mostly of fishing and agriculture.

Frequent questions about Taniti

  • What kind of power outlets are used?: The power outlets are standard to the US Outlet (120 volts).
  • What are the drinking laws there?: Alcohol can only be served between 9:00 am until 12:00 AM (midnight), and you must be 18 to drink alcohol.
  • What is the most common language?: Although they have a native language, most young Tanitians speak fluent English.
  • Is there any hospitals?: Taniti has one hospital, and many clinics which employ many multilingual employees.
  • What holidays do they celebrate there?: Taniti has quite a few holidays that the island businesses and restaurants shut down for. Check the days you book to ensure no major holidays are occurring during your stay.
  • What currency does Taniti use?: Taniti's official currency is the US Dollar, however, many businesses will accept euros and yens, and also accept most major credit cards. Several banks on the island provide currency exchange.
